# Linkrowth — Content Strategy

**Series:** *From One Prompt to a Fully Architected Agent System*
**Pillar goal:** Build authority and become visible to **tech recruiters** and **potential leads/clients** by building an AI LinkedIn-growth agent **in public**.
**Platforms:** LinkedIn (primary — depth & authority) · X (secondary — velocity & dev community).

---

## 1. The strategic bet

Most AI content online is one of two things: hype takes, or 10-minute tutorials that bolt on a framework in line one. This pillar is neither. The differentiator is a single honest thesis:

> **Earn your complexity.** Start with one prompt. Add the next layer only when the current one *visibly fails* — on camera.

That thesis is your moat because it's rare, it's credible, and it's *teachable*. Every episode has a built-in dramatic beat (the **villain** — the failure that motivates the next layer), which is exactly what makes content stop the scroll.

**The recursive hook (lean on this):** the thing you're building is itself an *engagement-growth* agent, and this series is an *engagement-growth* play. The product dogfoods the strategy and the strategy markets the product. Say this out loud often — it's a memorable, sticky frame.

---

## 2. Who you're talking to (and who's watching over their shoulder)

| Audience | What they want from you | What "winning" looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Tech recruiters** (the real target) | Proof you can architect real systems, not just prompt | Profile views, search appearances, InMails |
| **Potential leads/clients** | Evidence you can ship AI that solves a business problem | DMs, discovery calls, "can you build this for us" |
| **Fellow AI builders** (your amplifiers) | Honest engineering, reusable lessons, code | Comments, shares, reposts → reach |

You write **for builders** (they generate the reach) **so that recruiters and clients see the authority** (they generate the outcomes). Don't write directly to recruiters — write to peers; recruiters judge you by how peers respond.

---

## 3. The content engine: one episode → a week of content

Each engineering milestone (one rung of the ladder) is an **Episode**. Each episode is atomized into 5–7 posts across the week and both platforms. This is the whole machine:

| Post archetype | Angle | Best platform | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flagship** | "Episode N: I added [layer]. Here's the before/after." | LinkedIn long-form + X thread | The narrative spine |
| **The Villain** | "I shipped X. Here's exactly how it broke." | Both | Failure = relatability + credibility |
| **The Decision** | "Why I did *not* add [framework/vector DB] yet." | LinkedIn | Contrarian authority, anti-hype |
| **The Artifact** | Screen recording / code snippet / diagram | X (visual-native) | Proof it's real, not slideware |
| **The Principle** | One generalizable lesson ("earn your complexity") | Both | Quotable, save-able, shareable |
| **The Poll / Open Question** | "How would *you* solve the next problem?" | LinkedIn poll + X | Engagement bait + seeds next episode + free research |
| **BTS / Bloopers** | What went sideways, time spent, dead ends | X | Humanizes, build-in-public culture |

**Rule of one:** every post makes exactly one point and ends with one clear next action (reply prompt, follow-for-next, or a soft CTA). Don't stack CTAs.

## 4. Platform playbook

### LinkedIn (authority surface — recruiters & clients live here)
- **Format:** Hook (line 1–2 must work alone) → short story → the lesson → soft CTA. Generous white space. 1 idea per post.
- **Carousels** for the architecture: the ladder diagram, before/after, "the 7 layers." Carousels over-index on saves and dwell time.
- **Hooks that fit this pillar:** "I gave an AI one prompt to grow my LinkedIn. It failed in a way I didn't expect." / "Everyone adds a vector database too early. Here's the test for when you actually need one." / "Week 3 of building an AI agent in public. Today it embarrassed me."
- **Timing:** Tue–Thu, ~8–10am your audience's time. **Comments in the first 60–90 min drive reach** — be present to reply.
- **Engagement is the algorithm.** This is where you *dogfood*: spend 20 min/day leaving the kind of substantive, authority-signaling comments your own agent is designed to write. Recruiters and clients read comments.

### X (velocity surface — dev/AI community, your amplifiers)
- **Format:** Threads for episodes (hook tweet → 1 idea per tweet → demo gif/screenshot → recap + follow CTA). Standalone tweets for principles/hot-takes.
- **Show, don't tell:** screen recordings of the CLI running, code diffs, terminal output. X rewards the artifact.
- **Reply game:** reply thoughtfully in AI-builder / agent-engineering circles daily. Quote-tweet relevant launches with your "earn your complexity" lens.
- **Timing:** more frequent, lower stakes. 1 thread/week + 4–7 small posts. Post during US dev-Twitter waking hours if that's your target market.

### Cross-posting (adapt, never paste)
- Same idea, native format. LinkedIn = story + lesson; X = thread + artifact. The flagship drops the **same day** on both, rewritten for each.
- Repurpose laterally: a LinkedIn carousel → an X image thread; an X demo gif → a LinkedIn "watch it run" post.

## 5. Cadence (sustainable, not heroic)

**Weekly rhythm:**
- **1 flagship episode** (the meat) — same week, both platforms.
- **2–3 supporting posts** (villain / decision / poll / artifact) spread across the week.
- **Daily engagement block** (15–30 min) leaving substantive comments — this is non-negotiable and is the literal subject of your project.

**Monthly:** 1 "season recap" post (what shipped, what you learned, what's next) + repurpose the best-performing post into a fresh format.

> Ship cadence honestly. If an episode takes two weeks to build, post the *building* (villain, decision, BTS) in the gap. The build-in-public process is content even when the milestone isn't done.

---

## 6. Growth mechanics & CTAs

- **Open-source the thin SDK** (GitHub repo, MIT). For recruiters/clients this is the single strongest authority signal — public, real, architected code. Link it in bio and pin it.
- **The spec as a lead magnet.** Your `EPISODE-00` bible is a giveaway: *"Comment 'BLUEPRINT' and I'll send you the full architecture spec."* On-brand because the whole project is about engagement.
- **Follow-for-next.** Every flagship ends with: "Following along? Episode N+1 drops next week — I'm adding [teased layer]." Creates a serialized reason to follow, not just like.
- **Waitlist / newsletter** for people who want the finished system or the deeper write-ups — converts followers into an owned audience (and a lead list).
- **Bio = positioning.** Make your LinkedIn headline and X bio state the one sentence: *"Building AI agents in public · [your niche]."* Recruiters search headlines.

## 7. What to measure (and what to ignore)

| Tier | Metric | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Outcome (track weekly)** | Profile views, search appearances, recruiter InMails, inbound DMs from leads | These *are* the goal |
| **Authority signal** | Saves, shares/reposts, thoughtful comments, quality of who engages | Predicts outcomes; who comments > how many |
| **Reach (directional)** | Impressions, follower growth | Useful trend, not the point |
| **Vanity (mostly ignore)** | Raw likes | Weak signal of authority or pipeline |

Watch *who* engages more than *how many*. Five comments from senior engineers and a recruiter beat 200 likes from bots. Keep a simple log of inbound recruiter/lead conversations attributable to a specific post — that's your real ROI.

## 8. First 30 days (concrete calendar)

**Week 0 — Premise (pre-build):**
- *LinkedIn flagship:* "I'm building an AI agent to grow on LinkedIn — in public, starting from a single prompt. Here's the problem and the plan." (Define the villain, tease the ladder.)
- *X thread:* same premise, dev-flavored, end with "follow to watch it evolve from 30 lines to a real system."
- *Poll:* "What's the hardest part of growing on LinkedIn as a technical person?" → research + engagement.

**Week 1 — Episode 1 (one prompt → one comment):**
- *Flagship:* the 30-line baseline. Show it run. Show the soulless comment it produces. Name the villain ("it has no idea if the post was even worth engaging").
- *Artifact (X):* screen recording of the CLI.
- *Decision (LinkedIn):* "Why I started with zero frameworks — and you should too."

**Week 2 — Episode 2 (voice):**
- *Villain → fix:* "Last week it sounded like everyone. This week I gave it my voice. Here's the diff."
- *Principle:* "Context beats cleverness: the cheapest upgrade to any AI agent."
- *Poll:* "Should an AI write in your exact voice, or a cleaner version of it?"

**Week 3 — Episode 3 (triage):**
- *Flagship:* "Good comments on the wrong posts is just noise. I taught it to choose." 
- *BTS (X):* the scoring rubric, what surprised you.

**Week 4 — Recap + tease the extension:**
- *Season-1 recap carousel* (LinkedIn): the ladder so far, before/after, lessons.
- *Tease (both):* "Next: it stops waiting for me to paste posts — it reads the feed." → drives follows into Episode 4.

---

## 9. Voice & guardrails

- **Be honest about failure.** The villain beat only works if the failures are real. Don't fake-struggle; don't over-polish. Authenticity is the authority.
- **Anti-hype is your brand.** When everyone's adding agents/RAG/MCP to everything, your "do you actually need this yet?" lens is differentiated and senior.
- **Teach, don't flex.** Every post leaves the reader with something reusable. Authority is a byproduct of usefulness.
- **Stay ToS-clean and say so.** The human-in-the-loop, no-scraping stance is a *feature* to talk about — it signals judgment, which is exactly what recruiters and clients are buying.
